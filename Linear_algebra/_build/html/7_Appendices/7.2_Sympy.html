SymPy commands#

The SymPy library can perform symbolic calculations.

from sympy import *

Matrices#

Define matrices#

A = Matrix([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
display(A)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 2\\3 & 4\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]
B = Matrix([[5, 6], [7, 8]])
display(B)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}5 & 6\\7 & 8\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Matrix addition#

AplusB = A + B
display(AplusB)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}6 & 8\\10 & 12\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Multiplication by a scalar#

Atimes3 = 3 * A
display(Atimes3)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}3 & 6\\9 & 12\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Matrix multiplication#

AB = A * B
display(AB)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}19 & 22\\43 & 50\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Matrix exponent#

Asquared = A ** 2
display(Asquared)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}7 & 10\\15 & 22\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Matrix transpoose#

Atranspose = A.T
display(Atranspose)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 3\\2 & 4\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Zero matrix#

zero = zeros(2, 3)
display(zero)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}0 & 0 & 0\\0 & 0 & 0\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Identity matrix#

I = eye(3)
display(I)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 0 & 0\\0 & 1 & 0\\0 & 0 & 1\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Determinant of a matrix#

detA = A.det()
print(detA)

-2

Adjoint matrix#

adjA = A.adjugate()
display(adjA)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}4 & -2\\-3 & 1\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Inverse matrix#

invA = A.inv()
display(invA)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}-2 & 1\\\frac{3}{2} & - \frac{1}{2}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Systems of linear equations#

A = Matrix([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
display(A)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 2\\3 & 4\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]
b = Matrix([5, 11])
display(b)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}5\\11\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Solution using matrix inverse#

x = A.inv() * b
display(x)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1\\2\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Forming an augmented matrix#

Ab = A.row_join(b)
display(Ab)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 2 & 5\\3 & 4 & 11\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Row reduction#

Abrref = Ab.rref()[0]
display(Abrref)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 0 & 1\\0 & 1 & 2\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Rank of a matrix#

rankA = A.rank()
print(rankA)

2

Vectors#

a = Matrix([1, 2, 3])
display(a)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}1\\2\\3\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]
b = Matrix([4, 5, 6])
display(b)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}4\\5\\6\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Vector magnitude#

anorm = a.norm()
display(anorm)
\[\displaystyle \sqrt{14}\]

Unit vector#

ahat = a / a.norm()
display(ahat)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}\frac{\sqrt{14}}{14}\\\frac{\sqrt{14}}{7}\\\frac{3 \sqrt{14}}{14}\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]

Dot product#

adotb = a.dot(b)
print(adotb)

32

Cross product#

acrossb = a.cross(b)
display(acrossb)
\[\begin{split}\displaystyle \left[\begin{matrix}-3\\6\\-3\end{matrix}\right]\end{split}\]
